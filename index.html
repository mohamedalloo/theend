Built on consent, not tricks.

Your parent is the storyteller, not the product. We hold ourselves to rules most of this industry hasn’t thought about yet:

  • The interviewer says it’s an AI assistant — on every single call. No pretending.
  • Your parent says yes on the recorded line before anything is kept. “Stop calling” means we stop, immediately.
  • The recordings belong to your family. Download everything, anytime. Delete everything, anytime.
  • We never clone or synthesize your parent’s voice. The archive is their real voice — that’s the point.
  • Nothing in the book is invented. Every line traces back to a recording you can play.

Questions families ask

Will my mom actually talk to an AI?

The first call is introduced by you — we never cold-call. The interviewer is honest about being an AI assistant, speaks slowly and warmly, and lets her set the pace. In services like this, elders regularly stay on well past the planned fifteen minutes: someone is finally asking — and actually listening.

What if she has a landline and no smartphone?

Perfect — that’s who we built this for. A regular phone call is the whole product. There is nothing to install, charge, log into, or remember.

Does the AI write the book?

The AI organizes; it never invents. Chapters are assembled from her actual words, and every passage links to the recording it came from. You approve the final proof before printing.

What happens to the recordings if something happens to her?

They’re yours, forever. The voice archive is the heart of this — years from now, your kids will be able to press play and hear their grandmother tell her own story.

Why is it called theend.ai?

Because every great story deserves a proper ending — written by the person who lived it, while there’s still time to ask.
